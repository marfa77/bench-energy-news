Global seaborne coal exports have declined for the third consecutive week, dropping 10% to 14 million tonnes, with significant regional variations driven by reduced Australian and Colombian shipments and increased Indonesian exports.

Market Overview: Declining Global Coal Exports

The global coal export landscape continues to exhibit volatility, with a persistent downward trend in weekly shipments reflecting complex market dynamics and regional supply chain challenges. The latest data from BigMint's vessel line-up reveals nuanced shifts across key exporting nations.

Regional Export Breakdown

  • Australia: Experienced a 15.7% week-on-week decline, with exports falling to 5.35 million tonnes. Key ports Newcastle (2.78 mnt) and Gladstone (1.31 mnt) remain primary shipment centers.
  • Indonesia: Demonstrated resilience with a 5.4% export increase to 5.46 million tonnes, highlighting its growing significance in global coal trade.
  • Colombia: Witnessed a sharp 37.4% export reduction to 0.41 million tonnes, signaling potential supply chain disruptions.

    • Import Dynamics

    China and India continue to play pivotal roles in global coal imports. China emerged as the largest destination for Indonesian coal at 1.25 million tonnes, followed closely by India at 1.09 million tonnes, underscoring the critical energy demand in these rapidly industrializing economies.

    Market Fundamentals and Challenges

    The persistent decline in global coal exports reflects broader market challenges, including energy transition pressures, environmental regulations, and evolving geopolitical landscapes. Reduced exports suggest potential oversupply concerns and moderate global energy demand.
