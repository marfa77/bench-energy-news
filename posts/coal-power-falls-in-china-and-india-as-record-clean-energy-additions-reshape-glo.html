AI Summary: Coal-based electricity generation in China and India fell in 2025, marking the first simultaneous decline in more than five decades. New analysis published by Carbon Brief shows that coal power output declined by 1.6 percent in China and by 3.0 percent in India during the year.
Coal Power Decline in China and India: A Turning Point in Global Energy

Historic Shift: Coal Power Generation Falls in China and India

A groundbreaking analysis reveals a significant transformation in Asia's energy landscape, with both China and India experiencing a simultaneous decline in coal-based electricity generation for the first time in over 50 years.

Key Findings

  • China's coal power output decreased by 1.6%
  • India's coal generation dropped by 3.0%
  • Total reduction: Approximately 115 terawatt hours

Market Implications

This decline signals a robust shift towards clean energy, highlighting massive investments in renewable infrastructure and changing global energy dynamics.

"The simultaneous reduction in coal power by these two major economies represents a critical inflection point in global energy transition."

Future Outlook

Analysts predict continued momentum in renewable energy adoption, with significant implications for global climate strategies and energy investments.
